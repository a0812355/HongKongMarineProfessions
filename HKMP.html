3. Seafood Processing Industry (水產加工產業)

Last updata, April 2, 2024

Job Description:

Hong Kong's seafood processing industry transforms raw fish and other marine resources into consumable and retailable products. This covers techniques including drying, salting, smoking, freezing, and packaging. The industry serves both local and international markets and is an important part of the city's food supply chain.

Background History:

Since Hong Kong's port opened in the mid-19th century, an aquatic product processing industry has steadily grown. Initially, it was mainly for local consumption, and the processing methods were simple, such as sun-drying, pickling, and other traditional ways. The aquatic product processing business began using more complex technology and equipment in the 20th century, as Hong Kong's economy developed rapidly and residents' food quality requirements grew. The use of refrigeration and freezing technologies has increased the shelf life and food safety of marine products, allowing Hong Kong's processed aquatic products to begin exporting to international markets. In the 21st century, with globalization and the internationalization of food safety standards, Hong Kong's aquatic product processing industry has to follow to more intense food safety rules and standards.

Job Requirements:

Careers in the seafood processing industry typically require knowledge of food safety, handling standards, and familiarity with processing equipment. The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU) established the Department of Food Science and Nutrition.

Relationship with other professions:

1. This industry collaborates with the aquaculture and fishing industries to get raw resources.

2. Logistics and transportation are also involved to ensure effective distribution, food science for product creation, and retail for ultimate consumer sales.

Problem encountered:

The seafood processing industry focuses on the preservation, safety, and improvement of seafood products. This is critical for increasing shelf life, decreasing waste, and ensuring that seafood consumed is contaminant-free and prepared according to health requirements.

Future development possibilities:

The industry has the potential for expansion by using more modern processing technologies, such as automation and traceability systems, to improve efficiency and product traceability. There is also the possibility of producing more value-added products aimed at health-conscious customers and improving global markets. Future development opportunities include sustainable packaging and reducing the carbon impact of processing techniques.

5. Marine Research and Management Industry (海洋科研與管理產業)

Last updata, April 2, 2024

Job Description:

The marine scientific research and management industry refers to the conduct of marine-related research, such as basic marine scientific research, marine resource management, etc. The main research topics of marine scientists are marine physics (ocean currents, tides, waves, climate, etc.), marine chemistry (salinity, organic matter content, heavy metal content, etc.), marine biology, and marine geology (seabed topography). The research and detection data they collected not only greatly enhanced people's knowledge of the physical, chemical, biological, geological and geophysical environment of the sea, but also provided innovative services through application and value-added marine databases, further promoting industry, government, and academia. It also establishes a cooperative structure and promotes local value and world-leading forward-looking research topics, promotes marine science education, and protects the marine environment.

Background History:

In 1972, Hong Kong established the Agriculture, Fisheries and Conservation Department (AFCD), which is responsible for managing and monitoring the marine environment, fisheries, and protecting marine biological resources. In 1998, the Hong Kong Society of Naval Architects and Marine Engineers (HKSONAME) was established to promote exchanges and cooperation in local and international marine sciences in Hong Kong. In 2001, the Hong Kong Society of Marine Biotechnology (HKSMarB) was established to promote marine biotechnology and biodiversity research in Hong Kong. In 2002, The University of Hong Kong established the Swire Institute of Marine Science (SWIMS), which is dedicated to studying issues such as marine ecosystems, marine pollution, and climate change. In 2019, the Hong Kong branch of the Guangdong Provincial Laboratory of Southern Marine Science and Engineering (Guangzhou) located at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology was officially established. It was established to promote collaboration in marine science and technology innovation, optimize the industrial structure of the regional ocean, and expand new areas of blue economic development. fields, and promote the development of the marine economy in the Greater Bay Area. In recent years, the concept of Blue Economy has emerged. Through the exploration of marine resources and the development of marine biotechnology, the sustainable use of marine resources can promote economic growth, improve lives and employment, and maintain the health of the marine ecosystem.

Job Requirements:

If people want to manage marine resources, they must first understand how the ocean works, and then explore, exploit and apply marine resources. Therefore, people need to have knowledge and comprehensive skills related to marine science, technology, sustainable development, conservation, policy and management, as well as international perspective and analysis capabilities. The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST) not only establishes the Department of Marine Science, but also co-organizes the "Master of Science in Global Marine Resources Management" bachelor's degree program with the School of Marine and Earth Sciences at the University of Southampton (UoS) in the UK, leading all parties to understand marine technology in different fields.

Relationship with other professions:

1. Shipbuilding and Maintenance Industry: In order to strengthen marine scientific research, carry out an energy reconstruction plan for marine research vessels, continue to strengthen our country’s marine detection capabilities, continue marine research and development work, and significantly enhance the capabilities of marine survey and research work and efficiency.

2. Aquaculture industry: The marine scientific research and management industry has the potential to promote the upgrading, transformation and sustainable development of fisheries, such as encouraging the development of deep-sea cage culture technology and improving production capacity and fishery product quality.

Problem encountered:

1.Marine and coastal ecosystems are facing many new problems that have not been recognized or understood, and each problem has the potential to affect marine biodiversity, pollution issues such as the impact of new biodegradable materials on the marine environment, and ecological damage Problems such as the impact of wildfires on coastal ecosystems, overfishing in mid-ocean waters, and the movement of marine species away from the equatorial tropics due to climate change or global warming have all in turn led to a decline in tropical biodiversity, resulting in some of the world's most marine biodiversity The richest areas, including the Coral Triangle, the Philippines, Hong Kong, Indonesia and Papua New Guinea, are also at risk of reduced species richness. Climate change has also caused problems such as ocean acidification, rising ocean temperatures, and rising sea levels, posing threats to marine biological resources and the ecological environment, and also posing difficulties to marine scientific research and management.

2.Public awareness and concern about marine science and conservation still need to be improved. Strengthening marine science education and publicity and improving public awareness and awareness of marine issues are challenges facing the marine scientific research and management industry.

Future development possibilities:

The development of new technologies, such as soft robots and upgraded seabed tracking systems, will help scientists better understand marine species and their distribution, and more effectively help the development of marine protected areas
